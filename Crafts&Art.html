Crafts & Art — Basotho Traditions

A curated showcase of Basotho crafts: woven baskets, pottery, beadwork, leatherwork and more. Click any card for quick resources.

Grass hats

Mokorotlo

Traditional Basotho hats are worn during sunny days and events.They made with grass by Weaving in a cone and then coiling the grass around, to resemble a pointed dome.

Technique: Coiling & shaping
Handmade • Durable
Beadwork

Beadwork & Adornment

Intricate beadwork decorates clothing, women's adornments and ceremonial items. Patterns and colors can indicate status, age group or family lineage.

Materials: Glass beads, thread
Pottery

Handbuilt Pottery

Potters shape clay using coil and slab techniques. Functional wares — cooking pots and storage jars — often have simple incised decoration and burnished finishes.

Finish: Burnished or slip-painted
Fabrics

Basotho Blankets & Weaving

The famous Basotho blanket is both practical against mountain cold and highly symbolic. Contemporary makers blend traditional motifs with modern textile techniques.

Use: Warmth & Symbolism
Basket Weaving

Baskets

Skilled baskets weaving produce-baskets in which a strong natural fiber is commonly used.

Durability: Built to last
Woodcarving

Functional & Decorative Carving

From utensils to carved ceremonial objects, woodworking traditions include geometric patterns and stylized forms rooted in local symbolism.

Tools: Adzes, knives, rasps
